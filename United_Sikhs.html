UNITED SIKHS

United Sikhs is a non-profit organization that focuses on civil and human rights, humanitarian aid, and disaster relief. It is also affiliated with the United Nations. The organization was founded in 1999 by three Sikh Americans and operates on an international scale. Its mission is to provide assistance to people in need regardless of their ethnicity, race, gender, nationality, or religious beliefs.

ABOUT UNITED SIKHS

UNITED SIKHS is an international, U.N.-affiliated, non-profit, non-governmental organization that provides humanitarian aid, education, and human development services to disadvantaged and minority communities worldwide. The organization is headquartered in New York and is registered as a tax-exempt, non-profit organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the U.S. Internal Revenue Code. It is also registered as a charity in the State of New York and in England and Wales under the Charities Act 1993 (Charity Number 111 2055). Additionally, UNITED SIKHS is registered as a non-profit NGO in Australia (ABN 24 317 847 103), a registered NGO in Belgium, a non-profit organization in Canada, a tax-exempt organization under section 80G of the Income Tax Act 1961 in Punjab, and under the French Association Law 1901. Furthermore, it is registered as a society under the Societies Registration Act 1860 in Khyber Pakhtoonkhwa, Pakistan, and as a registered society under the Registrar of Societies in Malaysia (registered as UNITED SIKHS Malaysia Humanitarian Aid Organisation- Regn No: PPM-015-14-06042015), and currently pending NGO registration in the Republic of Ireland.

OUR HISTORY

In 1999, UNITED SIKHS was founded by a group of Sikhs from the New York metropolitan area who came together to help with the socio-economic development of immigrant communities in Queens, New York. Currently, the organization is a grassroots initiative with chapters in America, Asia, and Europe, and it carries out projects aimed at the spiritual, social, and economic empowerment of disadvantaged and minority communities. OUR MISSION

The mission of UNITED SIKHS is to uplift, educate, protect and transform the lives of underprivileged individuals and minority communities impacted by disasters, whether natural or man-made, by addressing issues such as hunger, illiteracy, diseases, and violation of civil and human rights. Their sustainable programs are designed to empower people regardless of their color, race, religion, creed, gender, disability, or national origin, enabling them to become informed and vibrant members of society.

UNITED SIKHS believes that progressive and enlightened societies can be created by socially conscious groups of people who are committed to developing and directing human potential. The organization's work, efforts, and achievements are a testament to their faith in this vision. UNITED SIKHS is committed to informing, educating, and uplifting fellow human beings, as well as participating in cross-cultural and social dialogues to ensure that the benefits of democracy are realized by all.

UNITED SIKHS also serves as a networking platform for like-minded organizations to establish and nurture meaningful projects and dialogues, promoting harmony, understanding, and reciprocity in villages, towns, and cities. To achieve its objectives, UNITED SIKHS participates in coalitions that share a common vision, based on the belief that there is no greater endeavor than to serve, empower, and uplift fellow human beings. The organization's philosophy centers around an unwavering commitment to civic service and social progress for the common good.

OUR STORY

UNITED SIKHS was established in 1999 by a group of Sikhs from the New York metropolitan area to aid in the socio-economic development of immigrant communities in Queens, New York. Today, the organization has grown into a grassroots movement with over 560,000 followers on Facebook as of August 2021, and more than 30,000 on Twitter. With chapters in North America, Asia, Africa, Australia, and Europe, UNITED SIKHS promotes volunteerism (seva), provides humanitarian aid, and advocates for the spiritual, social, and economic empowerment of underprivileged and minority communities.

UNITED SIKHS is registered as a non-governmental organization (NGO) and charity in several countries, including the USA, where it is registered as a non-profit tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code and as a New York State charity. It is also registered as a charity in England and Wales under the Charities Act 1993, as a not-for-profit charity in Australia, as an NGO in Belgium, as a non-profit organization in Canada, and under the Societies Registration Act 1860 and as a tax-exempt organization under section 80G of the Income Tax Act of 1961 in India. In addition, UNITED SIKHS is registered under the French Association Law 1901, under the Societies Registration Act 1860 in Khyber Pakhtoonkhwa, Pakistan, and as a registered society under the Registrar of Societies in Malaysia. The organization is currently awaiting registration as an NGO in the Republic of Ireland.

EQUAL OPPORTUNITIES STATEMENT

UNITED SIKHS is dedicated to upholding diversity and equal opportunities for its employees, volunteers, and project beneficiaries. The organization firmly believes in promoting diversity and equality for the betterment of society by advocating for the elimination of discrimination based on factors such as age, color, race, religion or belief, caste, ethnic or social origin, gender, genetic features, disability, sexual orientation, or language. This will be achieved by promoting education in equality and diversity and encouraging attitudes, customs, and practices that support equality. The organization will leverage publications, media, public advocacy, and charitable support to help those who have suffered discrimination on any of the aforementioned grounds.

SIKH AID: GLOBAL DISASTER RELIEF

Humanitarian action aims to save lives, alleviate suffering, and preserve human dignity during and after crises caused by natural disasters or human-made events. Its goal is also to prevent such situations and enhance emergency preparedness. The Sikh Aid team is committed to providing non-partisan disaster relief services worldwide, offering both immediate aid and long-term rehabilitation to individuals and communities in need. Additionally, the team runs ongoing initiatives throughout the year to combat poverty and contribute to building a healthier, happier world.





EMERGENCY RELIEF EFFORTS UNDERWAY AMIDST MASSIVE FLOODS IN PAKISTAN

Massive floods in Pakistan continue to cause havoc and devastation, with nightmarish scenes being witnessed. The floods have resulted in the loss of at least 1000 lives and thousands more injured, affecting nearly 30 million people. Julien Harneis, the U.N. resident coordinator and humanitarian coordinator in Pakistan, has described the flooding and landslides as the country's "biggest challenge" in decades, causing widespread destruction.

Responding swiftly to the crisis, the UNITED SIKHS Pakistan team has deployed resources and teams to provide emergency relief in the affected areas. Our teams are on the ground in Charsadda, Khyber Pankhtunkhwa province, distributing supplies such as cooked rice, biscuits, bread, mineral water, clothes, and infant milk to those in rescue camps.

The floods have caused significant damage to homes, public infrastructure, and resulted in human and livestock casualties. According to the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA), over 218,000 houses have been destroyed, with 452,000 homes damaged. Additionally, nearly 2 million acres of crops and orchards have been affected, and around 800,000 livestock have been killed. The large number of carcasses and stagnant water are causing skin infections, diarrhea, and other diseases, highlighting the urgent need for medical aid to prevent and control disease outbreaks. Our team is setting up medical camps to provide assistance to those who are ailing.

The floods have also damaged infrastructure, with over 3000 km of roads and 145 bridges destroyed, which is hindering the delivery of humanitarian aid.

COVID-19 VACCINATION

The COVID-19 vaccine is designed to protect individuals from contracting the virus and developing symptoms. The vaccine works in conjunction with your immune system to ensure that it is prepared to combat the virus if you come into contact with it. It is crucial to receive the vaccine when you are eligible and to continue following preventative measures such as wearing masks, maintaining social distancing of at least 6 feet, avoiding large crowds, and washing your hands regularly. Halting a pandemic requires utilizing all available resources to promote the health and well-being of yourself, your family, your colleagues, and your community.

It is important to note that an individual’s immigration status does not impact their eligibility to receive the COVID-19 vaccine. Furthermore, the vaccine is provided free of cost, and insurance is not required to receive it. While some locations may ask for insurance information prior to scheduling an appointment, this is not mandatory.

At present, the CDC has authorized and recommends three vaccines to prevent COVID-19.

It is recommended to receive the COVID-19 vaccine even if you have previously contracted the virus or possess COVID-19 antibodies. This is because the vaccine is considered safe and may enhance the level of protection that your body has already developed.

Following vaccination, it is possible to experience certain side effects. These are common indications that your body is building immunity against the virus. While the side effects from COVID-19 vaccination such as chills, fatigue, fever, body aches, etc., may affect your ability to carry out daily activities, they are typically temporary and should subside within a few days.



















CALIFORNIA FIRES

The risk of fast-moving wildfires in Northern California has put firefighters on high alert, and critical fire weather warnings remain in effect. Currently, over 6,000 firefighters are working to contain 19 wildfires, with 12 of them classified as major incidents.

The focus of the UNITED SIKHS Bay Area chapter is to provide assistance to the homeless community living in shelters and open encampments, as well as to those affected by natural disasters such as fires, and those impacted by the COVID-19 crisis.

In September, the organization reached an important milestone, having touched more than 3,400 lives through their Seva initiative since May. During that month alone, they helped 826 people, and have invested a total of 229 volunteer hours, reaching 1,004 cumulative hours since May.

Thanks to their efforts, the organization was approached by Free Meals, an agency that partners with the United States Department of Agriculture and farmers to provide free produce and fruit to people in need. UNITED SIKHS takes these boxes of food and distributes them to various shelters and homeless encampments, providing much-needed support to those in need.

The organization was also approached by FEMA and the Red Cross to support the thousands of Northern California residents who have been left homeless by the wildfires. With the help of over 25 volunteers, they prepared and distributed 1,500 boxes of food over two weekends, touching the lives of almost 750 people in San Jose, Mountain View, Los Gatos, and Santa Cruz areas.

During September, they donated items worth $6,885, bringing the cumulative total of donations since May to $23,337. With the support of generous donors and volunteers across the Bay Area, they have been able to provide critical items to homeless shelters, safe houses, encampments, public housing, students, families, and police precincts. Starting with just a two-person team and a few volunteers, they have now expanded to encompass the entire Bay Area, with volunteers in every city. None of this would be possible without the support of their dedicated volunteers and donors.

TUTORING PROGRAMS

The COVID-19 pandemic has created numerous unprecedented challenges that require innovative solutions. UNITED SIKHS has stepped up to help solve these problems and further serve the communities it represents.

One area where we have noticed people struggling is with online education, particularly for K-12 students. Lockdowns across the United States have led to the closure of in-person education, making online education necessary. However, online education presents its own challenges as students are unable to receive the extra support they need from teachers, parents, and school-related programs that are typically administered in person.

To fill this gap, UNITED SIKHS has initiated various local programs, such as the Homework Help Club and the Cyber Security Crash Course. Initially designed as an in-person service, the Homework Help Club was adapted to serve the needs of kids through online delivery of the program. The aim of the program is to assist children from lower socio-economic backgrounds in completing their homework. These children often require additional support in finishing their work as their parents may not have the time or ability to help them, and the schools they attend often lack the resources to create programs to assist these children. Since the onset of the pandemic, enrollment in the program has grown as the gap in access has widened. This program is critical in addressing the needs of people across the country.

We have also started a new program called the Cyber Security Crash Course, a selective course that teaches young people the basics of cyber security and strategy, taught by South-Asian cyber security experts across the country. Our intention was to deliver this course in person, but due to the pandemic, we had to switch to online delivery. We quickly adapted the curriculum and piloted the program in Orange County, California, with twenty students enrolled. With the success of this program, we plan to expand it to serve other counties in California and eventually the entire country.

UNITED SIKHS is also in the process of launching the Khalsa Tutoring Program, a tutoring service aimed at serving those who cannot afford or do not have access to high-quality tutoring. This service will begin by focusing on basic subjects such as early math and reading, but we plan to expand to subjects such as Calculus, Statistics, Biology, Chemistry, and SAT and AP tutoring. Ultimately, our goal is for this tutoring program to become a community resource that will be helpful to anyone and everyone, regardless of age, race, ethnicity, gender, etc.

To continue growing these programs that serve the disadvantaged and introduce new programs to address the needs of the underrepresented, UNITED SIKHS requires support from the community.
