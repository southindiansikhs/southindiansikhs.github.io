HARMANPREET KAUR

Harmanpreet Kaur is a renowned professional cricketer from India who currently leads the Indian Women's National Cricket Team in all formats of the game. She is an all-rounder who plays a crucial role in the team. Kaur was honored with the esteemed Arjuna Award for Cricket in 2017 by the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports in India, recognizing her excellence in the sport.

Kaur created history in November 2018 by becoming the first woman from India to score a century in a Women's Twenty20 International (WT20I) match. She achieved another significant milestone in October 2019 during the series against South Africa, becoming the first cricketer, male or female, from India to play in 100 international Twenty20 matches. Her contributions to cricket have earned her immense recognition and admiration in India and across the globe.

EARLY LIFE

Harmanpreet Kaur was born on March 8, 1989, in Moga, Punjab, to Harmandar Singh Bhullar and Satwinder Kaur. Her father was a volleyball and basketball player, and her parents are baptized Sikhs. Her younger sister, Hemjeet, holds a post-graduate degree in English and works as an assistant professor at Guru Nanak College in Moga.

Kaur's interest in cricket developed after joining the Gian Jyoti School Academy, which was located 30 kilometers (19 miles) away from her home in Moga. She received training under Kamaldeesh Singh Sodhi. In the early days of her career, Kaur used to play with men. In 2014, she moved to Mumbai and began working for the Indian Railways.

Kaur was inspired by Virender Sehwag, and her father, who is now a clerk at a judicial court, was her first cricket coach. Overall, Kaur's family has been a great support to her in pursuing her cricketing dreams.

CAREER

KAUR BATTING FOR SYDNEY THUNDER DURING WBBL|02

Harmanpreet Kaur started playing international cricket at the age of 20, when she made her One Day International (ODI) debut against the Pakistan women's team in March 2009 during the Women's Cricket World Cup held at Bradman Oval, Bowral. During the match, Kaur bowled four overs, conceding only ten runs and also caught Armaan Khan off Amita Sharma's bowling. In June of the same year, she made her Twenty20 International debut against the England women's team during the 2009 ICC Women's World Twenty20 at County Ground, Taunton, scoring eight runs off seven balls in her debut match.

Her potential as a hard-hitter was showcased in a T20I game against the England women's team played in Mumbai in 2010 when she scored a quick-fire innings of 33. In 2012, Kaur was appointed as the captain of the Indian women's team for the Women's Twenty20 Asia Cup final against the Pakistan women's team. She made her debut as captain in this game, leading the team to victory as they defended a total of 81 runs.

In March 2013, Kaur was appointed as the captain of India's women's ODI team for the series against Bangladesh. During the series, she scored her second ODI century in the second match and ended up with a total of 195 runs at an impressive average of 97.50, in addition to taking two wickets.

She made her Test debut in August 2014 against England and in November of the same year, she took nine wickets in a Test match against South Africa, which helped India to win the match by an innings and 34 runs.

In January 2016, Kaur played a vital role in India's series victory in Australia, scoring a quick-fire 46 off just 31 balls in India's highest-ever chase in T20 internationals. Her good form continued in the 2016 ICC Women's World Twenty20, where she scored 89 runs and picked up seven wickets in four matches.

KAUR RECEIVING ARJUNA AWARD IN 2020

In June 2016, Harmanpreet Kaur achieved a milestone by becoming the first Indian cricketer to be signed by an overseas Twenty20 franchise. She was signed by the Women's Big Bash League champions, Sydney Thunder, for the 2016-17 season.

On 20 July 2017, Kaur played a memorable innings in the semi-final of the 2017 Women's Cricket World Cup against Australia in Derby. She scored an unbeaten 171 off just 115 balls, which is the second-highest score by an Indian woman in one-day internationals, only behind Deepti Sharma's 188. Kaur also holds the record for the highest individual score for India in Women's Cricket World Cup history. In fact, her 171* is the highest ever individual score in a knockout stage of a Women's World Cup match, surpassing the previous record of 107* by Karen Rolton.

Kaur's performance helped India reach the final of the 2017 Women's Cricket World Cup, where they lost to England by nine runs. In July 2017, she became the second Indian batter to feature in the top-10 of ICC Women's ODI Player Rankings after Mithali Raj.

Harmanpreet Kaur's success continued in 2017 when she was selected as one of the players in the ICC Women's T20I Team of the Year in December. She was then appointed as the captain of India's squad for the 2018 ICC Women's World Twenty20 tournament in the West Indies in October 2018. Her outstanding performance in the tournament included becoming the first Indian woman to score a century in WT20Is in the first match against New Zealand, making 103 runs from 51 balls. She was the highest run-scorer for India in the competition, scoring 183 runs in five games.

In November 2018, Kaur was selected to play for Sydney Thunder in the 2018-19 Women's Big Bash League season. She continued to lead the Indian team and was chosen as the captain for the 2020 ICC Women's T20 World Cup in Australia. In 2021, Kaur was drafted by Manchester Originals for the first season of The Hundred. However, she played in only three games, scoring 104 runs before withdrawing due to an injury.

In March 2021, Harmanpreet Kaur achieved a significant milestone by becoming the fifth Indian woman cricketer to play in 100 ODIs for her country in a match against South Africa. In May 2021, she was appointed as the vice-captain of India's Test squad for their one-off match against the England women's cricket team.

In September 2021, Kaur was signed by Melbourne Renegades to play in the 2021-22 Women's Big Bash League season. In January 2022, she was selected to represent India in the 2022 Women's Cricket World Cup held in New Zealand. In July 2022, she was given the honour of captaining India's team for the cricket tournament at the 2022 Commonwealth Games in Birmingham, England.

In February 2023, Kaur was acquired by the Mumbai Indians for a price of ₹1.80 crores in the first edition of the WPL auction.

ACHIEVEMENTS

Harmanpreet Kaur has numerous achievements in her cricket career. Here are some notable ones:

 In June 2009, she made her Twenty20 International debut in the 2009 ICC Women's World Twenty20 against England women's at County Ground, Taunton where she scored 8 runs off 7 balls.

 She played a quick-fire innings of 33 against England women's in a T20I game played in Mumbai in 2010, showcasing her ability to hit the ball a long way.

 In 2012, she was named as Indian women's captain for the 2012 Women's Twenty20 Asia Cup final, and under her captaincy, India won the cup by defending 81 runs against Pakistan women's.

 In March 2013, she was named ODI captain of India women's and scored her second ODI century in the second match against Bangladesh women's in the same year. Kaur finished the series with 195 runs at an average of 97.50, along with a century and a fifty, along with 2 wickets.

 In November 2014, she took 9 wickets in a Test match against South Africa women's cricket team played at Gangotri Glades Cricket Ground, Mysore and helped India win the match by an innings and 34 runs.

 On 20 July 2017, she scored an unbeaten 171 runs off 115 balls against Australia in the semi-final of the 2017 Women's Cricket World Cup, which is currently the second-highest score by an Indian batter in women's one-day internationals.

 In December 2017, she was named as one of the players in the ICC Women's T20I Team of the Year.

 In October 2018, she was named as the captain of India's squad for the 2018 ICC Women's World Twenty20 tournament in the West Indies. In the opening match of the tournament, against New Zealand, she became the first woman for India to score a century in WT20Is, when she made 103 runs from 51 balls. She was the leading run-scorer for India in the tournament, with 183 runs in five matches.

 In January 2020, she was named as the captain of India's squad for the 2020 ICC Women's T20 World Cup in Australia.

 In 2021, she was drafted by Manchester Originals for the inaugural season of The Hundred. She played for them in 3 games, scoring 104 runs before withdrawing herself from the tournament due to injury.

 In July 2022, she was named as the captain of India's team for the cricket tournament at the 2022 Commonwealth Games in Birmingham, England.

 In February 2023, she was bought by Mumbai Indians for ₹1.80 crores in the inaugural WPL auction.
