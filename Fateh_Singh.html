CAPTAIN FATEH SINGH

Sardar Captain Fateh Singh was a Sikh soldier who served in the British Indian Army during World War II and was honored as a Mentioned in Despatch holder. He was born into a Labana Sikh (Ghotra) family in Perowshah, District Gujrat (now in Pakistan) and later settled in Loton, District Ambala, Haryana.






FAMILY AND EARLY LIFE

Sardar Captain Fateh Singh was born to Subedar Chattar Singh and Mata Bholi Kaur. His grandfather was Sardar Sona Singh. He attended a village school where he completed his lower middle school education up to 4th standard. As there was no school in his home village of Khori Duna Singh, he continued his studies at Guru Gobind Singh Khalsa Lobana High School in Tanda, Pakistan. He completed his matriculation (10th standard) from there in 1938 and learned Punjabi, Hindi, Urdu, Roman Urdu, and English.

His father, Subedar Chattar Singh, fought in World War I and was awarded three generation jangi inaam and OBI. His elder brother, Sardar Nand Singh, was awarded the IDSM and received 1 Murabba of land. Sardar Captain Fateh Singh was married to Bibi Pritam Kaur , daughter of late Subedar Major Budh Singh, and they had four children - two sons and two daughters.

MILITARY CAREER

On 29 July 1940, Sardar Captain Fateh Singh joined the 10/8th Punjab Regiment in Lahore. Before the partition of India, there were six groups of Punjab regiments, with 25% Sikhs, 50% Muslims, and 25% Hindus. Fateh Singh was posted with 33 Labana Sikhs in the 2/8 Punjab Regiment after taking the oath. This regiment was stationed at Chitral, near Tibet, where Labana Sikhs were posted due to a shortage of Jatt Sikhs. During World War II, Chitral was also known as Kala Pani (Black water), where prisoners were kept. Captain Saab met prisoners there. He was posted to the Signal Platoon HQ Company, where his friends Sher Singh (Gujrat, Pakistan) and Joga Singh Gotpokar (Gurdaspur) were also posted. In 1941, he passed Romanized Urdu, Map Reading, and 1st class English. The Commanding Officer recommended him for the promotion of VCO.

SPIRITUAL AWAKENING

In 1935, Sardar Captain Fateh Singh began reciting Gurbani. One night, he had a dream where he saw his deceased father with Guru Nanak Dev at 3:00 am. Captain Saab had been concerned about his household affairs, but in his dream, Guru Sahib reassured him that he would soon be promoted to the rank of VCO. After four months, he was indeed promoted. However, when his Commanding Officer saw him, he remarked that he was too young for the job. But upon looking through Captain Saab's files and seeing that he came from a family with a tradition of service in the Army, with his father Subedar Chatar Singh being a veteran, the C.O. concluded that Captain Saab was well-suited for the job and would meet all the requirements and needs.

AFTER MATH

After being promoted to VCO, Captain Sahib was sent to Jhansi training school. He was then posted to IAOC at Jabalpur. In 1943, IAOC and EME separated from each other, and he was transferred to 85 Mobile Workshop at Poona. After six months, his unit moved across India from Poona to Patna Sahib, Haridwar, Tarveni, Calcutta, and finally arrived at Imphal. By then, World War II was in full swing, and the Japanese had conquered eastern Burma and occupied Kohima, surrounding the 33 corps of the British army in June 1944. The Japanese army had constructed strong bunkers for defense.

His unit, 85 Mobile Workshop (IEME), was stationed at Palel. Later, it was moved from Tumboo Road to Burma and was placed at Dweren Fort, one of the largest forts in Asia, in Mandalay, which was the headquarters of Burma in the winter season, and in summer, Memeyo was the headquarters.

For his gallant and distinguished service at Kohima, he was awarded the Mention in Dispatches in the war. Along with two others, one NCO and one Sepoy, he was sent on a reconnaissance patrol by BDE/hq. During the patrol, they engaged a superior Japanese patrol, which they defeated. His brave actions that day played a significant part in the patrol's success. During WWII, his battalion was under siege by the Japanese at Kohima from March 23, 1944, to June 13, 1944. The war ended in 1945.

After the war, his unit moved to Shazanpur in UP. The Service Selection Board for commission selected him, but due to the reduction in army size, he was unable to avail of the chance of commission. He left the army on August 4, 1947. During his service, he had served in nearly all 'Class A establishments'.

REEMPLOYED

In 1949, Capt. Saab was re-employed as a JCO in Border Scouts. He took over the charge of the signal platoon in the First Scout Battalion and underwent infantry signal training that same year. In 1951, a reorganization took place, and MMG battalions were formed. Three border battalions (4, 5, and 6) were added to Mahar MMG Battalions 1, 2, and 3. An MMG special course was conducted by the Infantry College in Mhow, where Capt. Saab was selected as a Subedar Instructor in the MMG Division Weapon Wing at the Infantry College in Mhow.

Prior to joining the college, he served in the 7th Mahar MMG Battalion during the China Operation. His platoon's MMG was tasked with protecting the Sela Hill and providing cover fire to protect the Fourth Battalion of Sikh LI. Unfortunately, the Chinese firing resulted in the death of his platoon's Hawaldar and some jawans, but the Sela Hill was not surrendered. Later, while serving in the Fourth MMG Battalion, his platoon was at Kala Mor (Kargil), beyond Daras and Yo Zila Pass. His platoon's MMG was detailed to cover the movement of 101 Brigade and the Ninth Gorkha Battalion at Kargil.

He left the army in 1972 as a Subedar Major Honorary Captain.

CISF

After completing his 32 years of service in the army, he joined the Central Industrial Security Forces (CISF) in 1972 and served as an inspector for 10 years. He also served as an instructor at the CISF headquarters and centre in Hyderabad, as well as at Dhanbad coal mines, Mumbai and Nangal National Fertilizers Limited. While posted at CISF in Jharia, he effectively resolved various party disputes, theft cases, strikes of workers and coal blockades. He worked for more than a year in the coal mines of Bihar before being transferred to Hyderabad, then Mumbai, and finally to the National Fertilizers Limited in Nangal. In total, he served for 50 years.

SOCIAL ACTIVITIES

During the Second World War, a Gurudwara was constructed in Mandalay (Burma). Sardar Sant Singh, a renowned businessman, and his family met Captain Sahib and informed him about the destruction of their Gurudwara in the bombardment. They requested his help in reconstructing it. Captain Sahib, along with his friends, sought help from a British official who agreed to provide army trucks for transporting the construction material. Consequently, the Gurudwara was rebuilt.

After being posted in Mohw in 1964, he built a Gurudwara at the Infantry College (Mhow) and was appointed as its secretary. Meanwhile, Capt. Amrinder Singh, former Chief Minister of Punjab, was one of his students who contributed to the construction of the Gurudwara.

AFTER MILITARY SERVICE

• After retiring from CISF, he joined Swaraj Tractor factory as a manager and served for eight years. In 1988, he became the President of Gurdwara Toka Sahib. During his tenure, he oversaw the construction of the Langar Hall, Sarovar, Gobar plant, and road, among other projects. He served the Gurdwara with dedication and stepped down from his presidency in 2003.

• He is also the Regional President of Baba Makhan Shah Lobana Foundation, Chandigarh since 1997. He actively participates in various activities such as distributing scholarships, enlisting members of Labana Sewak, contributing to bima policies, and continues to work with the foundation.

• He played a significant role in extending the royal British grant for ex-servicemen who fought in 1939-45 and retired before 15th August 1947, including their widows. He continues to work in this department.

ACHIEVEMENTS

The achievements of Sardar Captain Fateh Singh:

 He served in the Indian Army for over 22 years, including during World War II, and received several medals and awards for his service.

 After retiring from the Army, he served for 10 years as an inspector in the Central Industrial Security Force (CISF), where he played a key role in resolving various disputes and strikes, as well as theft cases.

 He also served as an instructor at the CISF HQ and centers in Hyderabad (AP), Dhanbad coal mines, Mumbai, and Nangal (NFL).

 He participated in several courses and training programs, including Army Signal Course, QM Course, Catering Course, Wireless Course, MMG Course, Civil Defense Course, and Refinery Course, among others.

 He played a role in rebuilding a gurdwara that was destroyed during World War II in Mandalay (Burma), and also built a gurdwara at Infantry College (Mhow) during his tenure as secretary of the gurdwara.

 He served as the President of Gurdwara Toka Sahib, where he oversaw the construction of several facilities, including the Langar Hall, Sarovar, Gobar plant, and road.

 He served as the Regional President of Baba Makhan Shah Lobana Foundation, Chandigarh, where he participated in various activities like distribution of scholarships, making members of Labana Sewak, and contributions and bima policies.

 He played a key role in extending the royal British grant for ex-servicemen who fought in World War II and retired before August 15, 1947, including their widows, and still works in this department.
