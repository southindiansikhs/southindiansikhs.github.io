NISHKAM SWAT

Nishkam SWAT is a charitable organization based in West London. Its primary objective is to unite and transform financially disadvantaged communities by focusing on projects that make a difference to people's lives in the short term and improve their prospects in the long term.

FOUNDED IN 2008

Nishkam SWAT is a charitable organization based in West London with the primary aim of uniting and transforming financially disadvantaged communities. Their projects aim to make a positive impact on people's lives in the short and long term. One of their initial initiatives was to establish a youth club that conducts regular workshops on substance awareness, informal talks on the Sikh and other faiths, and encourages children to engage in group discussions and debates on current issues such as gang culture, drug addiction, and alcohol abuse.

In 2009, Nishkam SWAT discovered over 200 men sleeping rough on the streets of South all, which led to the establishment of their Homeless Project. They now go out to different areas of the UK to feed the less fortunate. Over time, Nishkam SWAT has launched many more projects.

THEIR VISION

Nishkam SWAT has a vision where every person they encounter feels loved and valued, lives in safety with respect, faith, hope, and care for themselves and others, achieving their full potential, and positively contributing to and transforming their communities.

Their work is based on the teachings of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the first Guru of the Sikhs, who taught the following principles:

1 Naam Japna - remembering God.

2 Kirat Karni - earning an honest living.

3 Vand Kay Shako - selflessly serving others, sharing income and resources.



NISHKAM SWAT'S WORK

Nishkam SWAT was founded in 2008 by Randeep Lall, and has since expanded to provide 4,000 meals per week for the homeless. The charity serves meals in several English towns, including London, Reading, Oxford, Bristol, and Luton, with volunteers including students and members of the interfaith community.

The charity was founded on the principle of Langar, which was started by Guru Nanak, and is the first charity to take Langar to the streets.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, they have worked with the NHS, government, and medical advisors to continue providing homeless feeding services.

Nishkam SWAT's work has been recognized in the Bollywood movie Street Dancer 3D.


HOMELESS PROJECT

In 2009, the trustees of Nishkam SWAT discovered that there were over 200 homeless people in their local area of Southall in Middlesex. This problem was complex and deeply rooted, involving various associated issues such as immigration status, addiction, alcoholism, and mental health. Nishkam SWAT had no previous experience in dealing with such issues, and therefore, it was all new territory for them. They explored every avenue available to them, including reaching out to the council, faith leaders, and the police. However, all roads eventually led back to them, and they realized that they had a shared responsibility to make a positive change in the lives of those living on the streets. They felt compelled to help as good citizens serving the communities they lived in. Ironically, they were unaware that this project would turn out to be their largest project! This marked the beginning of their Homeless Project.

This journey of compassion began when a homeless man extended his arm towards them for help. He then gave them exclusive access to a hidden homeless community of over 200 men of all ages living on the streets, under bridges, and in refuse collection rooms, all with different and complex issues. These men all had pride, and none of them wanted to be seen as victims. However, with homelessness comes a host of associated problems, often leading to a downward spiral without adequate care and rehabilitation. Over the next four years, Nishkam SWAT battled with local agencies ranging from the police to the council, immigration, health services, drug support charities, and others to provide support and care for this "forgotten community." Finally, in 2012, with the awareness raised from media outlets, including the BBC and national mainstream newspapers, the situation was at a place where Nishkam SWAT felt it was under control, with the problem reduced to just a handful of mainly men living on the streets in Southall, who were still being supported by their fully flourished network of over 150 volunteers.

Nishkam SWAT felt redundant at this point. They had built up a huge resource network from some major blue-chip organizations. Rather than calling them and saying that their work was over, they decided to take the message of their Guru and the ethos of their work to a wider audience in Central London. They started serving once a week on the Strand, which is a stone's throw away from the famous Trafalgar Square, among the hustle and bustle of office workers and tourists who could clearly see the need for the service, with Nishkam SWAT serving up to 300 people in a single session. They quickly added more outreach sessions and expanded their locations with the help of their dedicated volunteers, setting up outreach services for the homeless and less fortunate where the need was most. Today, Nishkam SWAT is blessed to be serving in 21 locations, 28 times a week, in various English towns, including London, Reading, Oxford, Bristol, and Luton, with volunteers comprising students and members of the interfaith community. They are an all-inclusive faith-based and value-led charity serving all of humanity regardless of caste, creed, colour, or faith.

NISHKAM SWAT PROJECT RECOVERY

Project Recovery was established in 2019 with the aim of providing support and guidance to individuals who are affected by alcohol or substance addiction. It is run entirely by volunteers and has the blessings of Waheguru Ji. The project seeks to raise awareness of addiction, encourage those who are suffering to seek help and provide a network of support for those in need.

In some communities, addiction is considered a taboo subject, which can make it difficult for individuals and their families to ask for help. Project Recovery aims to break down these barriers through an education program and by providing an easily accessible confidential helpline for those who need it.

The first step towards recovery is admitting to oneself that there is a problem. Project Recovery believes that everyone deserves the chance to recover and that every accomplishment begins with the decision to try.

The success of Project Recovery depends on the support and commitment of its dedicated volunteers. Nishkam SWAT is always looking for volunteers to get involved and help support the delivery of this project. They require volunteers for various roles, such as fundraising, marketing, project administration and helpline support.

Nishkam SWAT is also seeking individuals with experience or exposure in dealing with alcohol or substance addiction, who are willing to help others begin their journey to recovery.

ELDERLY CARE PROJECT

The Elderly Care Project was established in 2014 as a tribute to one of our youngest donors to Nishkam SWAT. Interestingly, it was one of our youngest supporters who inspired us to provide love, care and support for the oldest members of our community. The project is very close to our hearts, where volunteers gather to meet, care for and listen to elderly residents residing in care homes, some of whom have no surviving family or friends to visit them. Currently, the project focuses on visiting two care homes on a fortnightly basis on Saturday mornings. One of the care homes has residents who mainly suffer from dementia and can sometimes display challenging behavior. Volunteers spend time talking, listening, and reminiscing about fond memories with them.

Nishkam SWAT chose to undertake this service because the elderly, particularly those in full-time residential care, are often overlooked. Nishkam SWAT believed that caring for and respecting the elderly should be given priority, and wanted to raise awareness among others in our community about how lonely some residents are, often spending their days in silence. This raises the question of whether one day we may also find ourselves in full-time care and whether it wouldn't be nice to have someone visit us regularly. The residents express their gratitude and appreciation with beautiful thanks or smiles, akin to the love of a grandparent.

In the short term, our future aspiration is to increase the frequency of our visits from fortnightly to weekly sessions, and in the longer term, we hope to add more care homes to reach even more elderly hearts.

NISHKAM HEALTHCARE

The Ambulance Project was established in 2017 and has since flourished thanks to the efforts of our valued volunteers who took part in the annual Nishkam SWAT 26-mile charity walk and raised an impressive £40k to purchase our first fully functional ambulance. The ambulance has been a crucial tool in providing basic medical attention to the homeless and displaced on the streets. Every penny raised went towards paying for the ambulance and medical supplies to support the basic medical and dental needs of individuals living on the streets.

While these individuals can walk into an A&E department of a hospital for medical attention, they are often restricted by mental health issues and lack of a fixed abode. They are in a downward spiral on the streets and often neglect their physical health, with simple conditions going untreated, which can then develop into more serious and complex issues. During the winter months, Tesco Pharmacy has kindly donated flu jabs to be administered to those living on the streets. The ambulance is also used as a mobile dentist surgery, with dentists offering basic teeth checks and hygiene information, while distributing toothbrushes, toothpaste, and mouthwash.

In the coming months, Nishkam SWAT aspires to extend the ambulance provision to our other outreach locations, including Slough, Reading, Oxford, Colchester, and Brighton, while also addressing the mental health needs of those living on the streets. At the heart of all our projects is the encouragement for volunteers to serve wholeheartedly, selflessly, and without ego. Nishkam SWAT is a value-driven organization with kindness, care, and compassion at the core of all operations.
