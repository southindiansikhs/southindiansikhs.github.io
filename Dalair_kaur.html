BIBI DALAIR KAUR JI

The Mughal soldiers charged up the hill towards the fortress gates, with their heads down, one hand holding the horse reins, and the other hand wielding a sword, determined to break through. However, their efforts were met with a barrage of a thousand arrows and bullets raining down from the high fortress walls. Despite their best efforts, they were unable to penetrate Anandpur, the Khalsa fort.

"Sit down, generals," commanded the leader of the royal forces. A desperate general screamed "RETREAT! RETREAT!" as he witnessed hundreds of his imperial forces dropping to the ground. An emergency meeting was called back at base camp. The jungle nights were cold and hundreds of campfires were seen all around the hill's base. The Anandpur Fort stood atop, impregnable and untouched.

"We have been stuck in this wretched, mosquito-infested jungle for 9 long months. When will those Sikhs give up?" lamented Wajir Khan.

"Please be patient, General. Even the Sikhs will succumb one day," replied Jabardast Khan.

"How patient can we be? We have over 1 million soldiers out there. One million soldiers to kill Guru Gobind Singh. Yet, his Khalsa army has killed tens of thousands of our soldiers, and we have barely made a dent in the fortress walls," exclaimed Wajir Khan, his frustration evident.

"We agree that a new strategy is required, but what can we do differently?" asked Jabardast Khan.

"We must cut off their supplies, their food, and their water. We must starve them out," suggested an unknown general.

"But how do we do that? They have been preparing for this siege for months, and they have stockpiled enough supplies to last them for years," reasoned Jabardast Khan.

"Please take your seats, Generals. As the leader of the royal forces serving his excellency Emperor Aurangzeb, I will make the final decisions," declared Aurangzeb's General. "Let us review our tactics. We have 22 local Hindu kings supporting us, as well as the victorious armies of Wajir Khan and Jabardast Khan, and the military might of the Mughal Empire. We have one million soldiers, countless cannons, and an unlimited supply of arms. Fellow Generals, we have underestimated these Khalsa soldiers. Despite the fact that there cannot be more than 10,000 soldiers in the fort, we have not been able to defeat them through sheer force. They have the superior position on the hilltop. We need a new plan, another strategy."

"Patience is the best strategy. We have imposed the siege for several months now, and I believe that our siege is working, fellow Generals. We have cut off all supplies of food and water to the fort. Those soldiers must be close to starvation by now. If we wait for just a few more months, victory will be ours," suggested Jabardast Khan.

"It's already December, and a cold and miserable winter lies ahead of us. Our camp hospitals are overflowing with sick soldiers. The floods last month brought so many mysterious illnesses that hundreds of soldiers are dying every day. There is low morale among the troops, and thousands of soldiers are deserting us every week because they believe we will never be able to capture the Fort. Do any of us want to spend the rest of winter like this? No. I suggest we lure out the Khalsa Sikhs and finish them with our swords!" raged Wajir Khan. The room full of Generals stood up and cheered.

"Beloved son and Guru, please listen to your mother," said Mata Gujree. "You are beautiful and blessed by your father. Whatever you decide is law, but I urge you to reconsider the offer of safe passage from the Royal forces. Aurangzeb himself has given his word, promising on the Quran that no harm will come to us."

"Mata Ji, it seems like a mere deception. Why would they offer us safe passage after trying to kill us for 9 months?" questioned Guru Gobind Singh Ji.

"Guru Ji, you are our Father, and we live for you and will die for you," responded Bhai Mani Singh. "But Guru Ji, we began with 10,000 Khalsa warriors and now we have less than a thousand. Guru Ji, you are the King of this world and the next. It doesn't matter to us if we live at your feet in this world or the next, but Guru Ji, this world needs you. You must survive. If you stay here, you will die with the rest of us."

"O Beloved Khalsa Ji, I would rather die fighting than walk away from this battle. But Khalsa Ji, you are my Guru and you have ordered that we accept the offer of safe passage and leave the fort. So we will leave tomorrow. But, I don't intend to leave the fort unguarded. Bibi Dalair Kaur Ji, you are my trusted Sikh. I want you and the rest of my daughters to stay here along with 10 Khalsa men," declared Guru Gobind Singh Ji.

"Pita Ji, your words are true. Bless us so we may do this duty and bring honour to the Khalsa," replied Bibi Dalair Kaur Ji.

After bidding farewell to Bibi Dalair Kaur Ji and leaving the fort, Guru Gobind Singh Ji and his 500 Khalsa soldiers were pursued by the Moghal troops and Hill Rajas, who had broken their sacred oath of safe passage. A fierce battle ensued on the banks of the Sirsa river, resulting in the death of many Khalsa soldiers and the scattering of Guru Ji's family. His younger sons and mother escaped with Gangu to his village, where he later betrayed them to the authorities for a reward, leading to their martyrdom.

Bhai Mani Singh and Guru Ji's wife managed to escape and rode to Delhi. Meanwhile, Guru Gobind Singh Ji, his two older sons, and the remaining 40 Khalsa soldiers escaped to Chamkaur where they fought another battle against the odds. All 40 Khalsa soldiers, including the older sons, fought bravely to the death. The Khalsa urged Guru Gobind Singh Ji to survive, and he escaped to Mashiwadha.

After some of the Moghul forces chased after the Khalsa, the rest of the army rode to the fort to claim victory. Bibi Dalair Kaur, realizing that her Guru and Khalsa had been betrayed, prepared Guru Gobind Singh Ji's daughters for battle. As the Moghul soldiers rode towards the fort, burning and looting everything in their path, Bibi Dalair Kaur stood before the Guru's daughters and spoke with passion, "Sisters, we have given our heads to our Guru-Father at the Amrit ceremony. We have lived for Truth, and now the time has come to die for it. Sisters, remember that we are all trained warriors, and we will die fighting rather than be taken as slaves. Sisters, pick up your guns and get in position. It's a good day to die! BOLAY SO NIHAL! SAT SRI AKAL!"

The enemy soldiers, thinking that the fort was empty, were taken by surprise by the sudden hail of bullets. Within minutes, hundreds of dead soldiers lay outside the fort. Seeing this, the remaining soldiers left their positions and fled for their lives. Wajir Khan, enraged at not having captured the fort, screamed at his men to fire the cannons. Intense cannon fire succeeded in breaking through a wall of the fort, but no one could be seen inside. As the ground troops charged towards the fort, they were suddenly showered with bullets, and line after line of soldiers fell dead to the ground.

Wajir Khan was relentless and continued to send more troops to the fort. Eventually, the Khalsa women ran out of bullets, and Wajir Khan was pleased that he would soon capture them.

Bibi Dalair Kaur Ji gathered her sisters together and spoke with her Guru-Father's blessing, saying, "Sisters, we have fought bravely, and now it is time for us to die with honor. We must remember the courageous women from Rajput. When their husbands died in battle and their fort was about to be captured, they would all jump into a fire and burn to death rather than let the enemy dishonor them. This was how their religion taught them to preserve their honor. Our Guru-Father is always with his Sikhs and protects his sons' and daughters' honor. Sisters, our Guru-Father has trained us as warriors, and we will die fighting alongside our husbands and brothers. Remember, we are lionesses."

As the enemy troops rapidly approached, there was no time left for speeches. Bibi Dalair Kaur Ji gave the signal and all the Khalsa women drew their swords and took their positions behind the damaged wall, which was the only way for the enemy to enter.

The mighty soldiers started climbing over the piles of rubble, but when they saw that there were only 100 Khalsa women and 10 Khalsa men ready for battle, they stopped in their tracks. They had expected to find hundreds of Khalsa men, and were surprised to see women warriors.

From a distance, Wajir Khan watched the events unfold and yelled, "Cowards, are you afraid of women? They are gifts for you, capture them and do what you want with the rewards of your hunt." Bibi Dalair Kaur yelled back, "We are the hunters, not the hunted. Come forward and find out for yourself!"

Taking up the challenge, Wajir Khan rode into the fort with his men, but the Khalsa Lionesses attacked them from every corner and he fell dead to the ground. Not knowing how many other Khalsa warriors were in the fort, the Moghuls retreated once again.

Jabardast Khan was shouting orders to his men to fire the cannons. The powerful cannon fire destroyed the already weakened wall of the fort. From the outside, the inner compound of the fort was now clearly visible, but there were no Khalsa warriors in sight. Jabardast Khan assumed that they must have all been killed and decided to take thousands of troops with him into the fort. They searched every corner of the fort but could not find anyone. Jabardast Khan was enraged and ordered his men to find the Khalsa warriors. The soldiers searched carefully, expecting a surprise attack at any moment. Eventually, they came to the conclusion that the remaining Khalsa warriors had escaped through a secret passage.

Orders were given to abandon the search and begin looting the fort. The same soldiers who were previously afraid for their lives began searching for wealth inside the fort. However, when they removed the rubble from the fallen wall, they found no wealth, only the bodies of the Khalsa martyrs. The faces of Guru Gobind Singh Ji's daughters still radiated with peacefulness.

These Khalsa warriors, along with their brothers and husbands, had fought bravely alongside their leader, Bibi Dalair Kaur. They had died fighting to the death and earned an eternal place at the feet of our Guru-Father.

The passage "Dayh Shiva bar mohe ehai..." is a hymn written by Guru Gobind Singh Ji. It requests a boon from the Lord to never refrain from righteous deeds, to have no fear when fighting the enemy, and to attain victory with faith and fortitude. The hymn also expresses a desire to keep the teachings of the Lord close to one's mind and to sing His praises. It concludes with a request to die fighting with limitless courage in the battlefield.
