RANDHIR SINGH

Randhir Singh, born on October 18, 1946, is a former Olympic-level trap and skeet shooter and an Indian sports administrator. He is widely known for his extensive international connections and is one of the most influential sports administrators in India. Singh has held several positions throughout his sports administration career. Currently, he serves as the Acting President of the Olympic Council of Asia (OCA), which he began on September 11, 2021.

Singh has also served as a member of the International Olympic Committee (IOC) from 2001 to 2014 and since 2014, he has held the position of honorary member of the IOC. Furthermore, he has held the role of Secretary General of the OCA from 1991 to 2015. In domestic sports administration, Singh has held various positions, including the Secretary General of the Indian Olympic Association (IOA) from 1987 to 2012 and a member of the governing board of the Sports Authority of India from 1987 to 2010.

During his shooting career, Randhir Singh competed in five Olympic Games and became the first Indian shooter to win a gold medal at the 1978 Asian Games. In recognition of his achievements in shooting, he was awarded the Arjuna Award in 1979. Singh began his sports administration career in 1984 while still an active shooter, but retired from shooting in 1994.

BACKGROUND AND EARLY LIFE

Randhir Singh was born in Patiala, Punjab Province, British India on 18 October 1946. He comes from a family of influential sports administrators. His uncle, Maharaja Yadavindra Singh, played a key role in lobbying for and organizing the first Asian Games in 1951 in Delhi, India. His father, Bhalindra, was a member of the IOC from 1947 to 1992, President of the Indian Olympic Association from 1960 to 1975 and 1990 to 1984, and was instrumental in bringing the 1982 Asian Games to Delhi. Singh completed his education at Yadvindra Public School in Patiala and graduated from St. Stephen's College, Delhi, with a Bachelor of Arts degree in History.

SHOOTING CAREER

Randhir Singh began his senior shooting career at the age of eighteen, participating in the Indian National Championships in 1964, where he was a member of the winning trap shooting team. The team defended their title the following year, and Singh won his first national individual title in 1967 in skeet shooting. Over time, he won numerous national titles in both skeet and trap shooting. Singh went on to become the first Indian shooter to win a gold medal at the Asian Games, which he accomplished during the 1978 Asian Games in Bangkok, Thailand. In 1979, he received the Arjuna Award in recognition of his achievements in shooting. At the 1982 Asian Games held in Delhi, India, Singh and his team won a silver medal.

From 1968 to 1984, Singh participated in five Olympic Games in mixed trap shooting. He was the second Indian, after Karni Singh, to compete at five Olympics. His best performance was 17th place at the 1968 Olympics, where he missed the bronze medal by four points. He also competed in four Asian Games and won medals of each color. His last international competition was the 1994 Asian Games in Hiroshima, Japan. Singh held the position of Secretary General of the Olympic Council of Asia (OCA) from 1991, and he became the first person to participate in the Asian Games while holding an office in a continental sports organization.

SPORTS ADMINISTRATOR

Singh is widely recognized as one of the most influential sports administrators in India, and his impact on the International Olympic Committee (IOC) was significant during his tenure as a full member. Singh first entered the world of sports administration in 1984, when he was elected as a joint secretary to the Indian Olympic Association (IOA). In 2001, during the 112th IOC session held in Moscow, Russia, Singh was elected to the IOC with 101 votes, the highest number of votes received by any candidate. He served as a member of the IOC from 2001 to 2014, and has been an honorary member since 2014.

Singh has an extensive record of serving as a sports administrator. He was the Secretary General of the IOA from 1987 to 2012, a member of the Sports Authority of India's governing board from 1987 to 2010, and the Secretary General of the OCA from 1991 to 2015. He was also a member of the WADA Foundation Board from 2003 to 2005 and has been a member of the ANOC Executive Board since 2002.

Singh has served on several commissions, including the Olympic Games Study from 2002 to 2003, Sport for All since 2004, Women and Sport since 2006, Coordination for the 1st Summer Youth Olympic Games in Singapore in 2010, and the Olympic Truce Foundation since 2007. He was the Founder Secretary General of the Afro-Asian Games Council from 1998 to 2007, and played a crucial role in organizing the only Afro-Asian Games in Hyderabad in 2003.

In addition, Singh was the Secretary General of the OCA from 1991 to 2015, the Life Vice President of the OCA from 2015 to 2021, and is currently serving as the Acting President of the OCA since 2021.

2010 COMMONWEALTH GAMES

Singh played a key role in bringing the 2010 Commonwealth Games to India and held the position of Vice Chairman of the Organizing Committee. He was the only senior official with a clean image, amidst controversy surrounding the committee. However, during the planning of the games, there were reported clashes between Singh and Suresh Kalmadi, who was the President of the IOA at the time. The clashes became so severe that Mike Fennell, the head of the Commonwealth Games, had to inform the then-Prime Minister of India, Manmohan Singh.

OLYMPIC COUNCIL OF ASIA

Singh held the position of Secretary General of the OCA from 1991 to 2015, and in 2019, he was appointed by the General Assembly of the OCA as the chairman of the coordination committee for the 2022 Asian Games, which will take place in Hangzhou, China.

ACTING PRESIDENCY (2021-PRESENT)

In September 2021, Singh assumed the role of Acting President of the OCA, which is headquartered in Kuwait City. His appointment followed the resignation of Sheikh Ahmed Al-Fahad Al-Ahmed Al-Sabah, who stepped down as president after being found guilty of forgery by a court in Geneva. In May 2022, following a meeting of the OCA Executive Board in Tashkent, Uzbekistan, Singh announced that the 2022 Asian Games, scheduled to be held in Hangzhou, China, had been postponed to 2023 due to complications related to the COVID-19 pandemic in China.

During the OCA General Assembly meeting in October 2022, held in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, Singh addressed the attendees, stating that the OCA has now returned to a normal state following the COVID-19 pandemic. At the same event, Singh, among others, signed the contract to host the 2029 Asian Winter Games, which will take place in Trojena, located in NEOM, Saudi Arabia. In addition, Singh was a speaker at the OCA's inaugural Gender Equity Seminar in Manama, Bahrain, also held in October 2022.

AWARDS AND ACHIEVEMENTS

Randhir Singh has received several awards and achievements throughout his career in sports administration. Here are some of them:

In 2012, Singh was awarded the Olympic Order, the highest award of the Olympic Movement, by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) for his contribution to the development of sport and the Olympic Movement.

In 2016, Singh was awarded the Association of National Olympic Committees (ANOC) Order of Merit for his services to sport.

In 2004, Singh was awarded the Padma Shri, one of the highest civilian awards in India, for his contribution to sports administration.

In 2009, Singh was awarded the Olympic Council of Asia's Merit Award for his contribution to the development of sports in Asia.

In 2021, Singh was appointed as the Acting President of the Olympic Council of Asia, a prestigious position in sports administration.

Singh has also served as a member of various important committees and commissions of the IOC and the ANOC, including the Olympic Games Study, Sport for All, Women and Sport, and Coordination for the 1st Summer Youth Olympic Games in Singapore.
