BIBI RAJINDER KAUR JI

Bibi Rajinder Kaur, also known as Rajindan, was a courageous princess from Patiala and the granddaughter of Baba Ala Singh. Her father, Bhumla Singh, passed away when she was just four years old, and she was raised by her grandfather. In 1751, she got married to Chaudhari Tilok Chand of Phagwara, but unfortunately, her husband passed away at a young age. This left Rajindar Kaur in charge of the family estate, which consisted of over two hundred villages.

In 1765, Baba Ala Singh was arrested by Ahmad Shah Durrani for not paying the tribute, and he was being taken to Lahore. Rajindar Kaur went to her grandfather and offered to pay the money to secure his release. However, he declined her offer, saying that it was not proper to accept money from a family member.

In 1778, Rajindar Kaur's first cousin, Raja Amar Singh of Patiala, was defeated by Hari Singh of Sialba. Rajindar Kaur came to his rescue with three thousand soldiers, marching through the territories of the chiefs who had fought on the side of Raja Amar Singh.

During the reign of the minor Raja Sahib Singh, Rajindar Kaur was in Patiala again to defend the town against Maratha attacks. She marched with a strong force as far as Mathura, where peace talks were opened with the Marathas.

Bibi Rajindar Kaur passed away in 1791 in Patiala after a short illness. She was cremated in the royal cemetery known as Shahi Samadhan.

According to Sir Lepel Griffin, the author of "The Rajas of the Punjab," Rani Rajinder Kaur was one of the most remarkable women of her time. She possessed all the virtues that men typically attribute to themselves, such as courage, perseverance, and sagacity, without any of the weaknesses that men typically attribute to women.

Rani Rajinder Kaur was given the title of "Rani" or "Queen" due to being the granddaughter of Baba Ala Singh, a famous ruler of the Patiala State. She was the only child of her father Bhuma Singh, who died in 1742 when she was just four years old. She was brought up by her grandmother Bibi Fatto.

At the tender age of 13, she got married to Tilok Chand, the chief of Phagwara, which is a city near Jullundur. Unfortunately, her husband passed away at a young age, and she took charge of the family estate, which consisted of approximately two hundred villages. Despite her young age, she managed the estate efficiently.

In 1765, Baba Ala Singh was arrested by Ahmad Shah Durran as he had failed to pay his tribute regularly. DurBibi wanted to collect the entire amount in one installment and took the Baba to Lahore. Rajinder Kaur, his granddaughter, offered to pay the amount to secure his release, but Baba Ala Singh refused, stating that it was not appropriate to accept money from a family member. Later, his wife managed to secure his release.

In 1778, Amar Singh, the ruler of the Patiala state, was defeated by Hari Singh, chief of Sailba. Rajinder Kaur, who was Amar Singh's first cousin, could not bear the humiliation and decided to help him. She also had a personal grudge against Hari Singh, who had eloped with a lady from her family. According to historian Hari Ram Gupta, Rajinder Kaur was a great commander, and with a force of 3000 men, she marched from Phagwara to Patiala. She ravaged the country of Hari Singh and his supporters along the way. Hari Singh was defeated, and he had to pay a large tribute to Amar Singh.

Amar Singh was succeeded by his six-year-old son Sahib Singh in 1781. During his minority, his grandmother Bibi Hukman appointed Nanum as his Prime Minister. However, the situation in the state was delicate, and there were intrigues and conspiracies all around. The state was on the verge of disintegration into smaller states, but it was saved by the able and faithful Nanum Mal, who was severely wounded in an engagement.

In 1783, the ladies of the imperial household put Nanum Mal into prison and started plundering the treasury. As a result, Sikh chiefs seized as many villages of the state as they could.

Rajinder Kaur refused to accept the downfall of her family's state. She raised an army and hurried from Phagwara to Patiala to rescue Nanum Mal and reinstate him as the Prime Minister in 1785. Together, they, along with the help of Dhar Rao, a Maratha chief, recaptured the lost territories of the state and reestablished Sahib Singh's rule. As a token of appreciation, the Maratha chief was awarded a substantial amount of money.

In 1790, Rana Khan, another Maratha general, marched towards Patiala and demanded eight hundred thousand rupees as tribute. Bibi Rajinder Kaur could not tolerate this, but also realized that her state could not win against the large Maratha army. Therefore, she led a military guard and, along with Nanum Mal, visited the Maratha camp. She convinced Rana Khan to retreat and promised to pay the amount after personally settling the issue with his chief, Mahadiji Sindhia, who was camped at Mathura. She marched all the way to Mathura and was treated with great respect. She resolved the issue with Sindhia and returned after settling the amount.

In Rajinder Kaur's absence, other ministers and chiefs conspired against her and Nanum Mal, poisoning Sahib Singh's mind. Unfortunately, Sahib Singh fell for their schemes, leading to the dismissal of Nanum Mal and ultimately the downfall of Rajinder Kaur. When she returned from Mathura after four months, she requested a meeting with Sahib Singh, but he declined and all her privileges were withdrawn. She lost all the respect and esteem she had previously earned, and this ingratitude for her selfless service to the state shattered her. Disgraced, she fell ill in Patiala and died in 1791 after a brief sickness. She suffered greatly due to the foolishness of Sahib Singh and left the world in despair.
