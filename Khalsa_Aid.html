KHALSA AID

Khalsa Aid is a UK-based international non-profit humanitarian organization that was established in 1999. The organization is dedicated to providing assistance to victims of both natural and man-made disasters across the globe. Khalsa Aid has received recognition for its contributions in various relief efforts, including helping those affected by the 2016 London floods, providing aid in war-torn Syria in 2015, setting up refugee camps for Rohingya Muslims on the Bangladesh-Myanmar border in 2017, aiding in the reconstruction of Kerala following the 2018 floods, and supporting NHS workers in the UK and the impoverished in India during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Khalsa Aid is led by a team of key individuals who are dedicated to its mission of providing humanitarian aid to those in need. These individuals include:

 Ravi Singh, who is the founder and CEO of Khalsa Aid.

 Amarpreet Singh, who serves as the India Director.

 Japneet Singh, who holds the position of Treasurer.

 Jindi Singh, who serves as the Canada Director.

 Omar Singh, who is the USA Director.

 Bal Sandhu, who serves as the General Secretary.

 Jaswinder Singh, who holds the position of Treasurer.

 Amarjit Singh Bansal and Tarvinder Singh, who both serve as Trustees.

 Indy Singh Hothi, a former Trustee and Director.

Together, this team works tirelessly to ensure that Khalsa Aid can provide essential aid to those in need across the world.

ABOUT KHALSA AID

Khalsa Aid International is a UK-based humanitarian relief charity that was established to provide aid and support to those affected by natural and man-made disasters such as floods, earthquakes, famine, and war. Their team is often one of the first on the scene to distribute food, water, clothing, medical and sanitation supplies. They fund and build semi-permanent shelters, if necessary, to help save lives, reduce people's suffering, and maintain their dignity. The charity was founded by Ravinder (Ravi) Singh, who was moved by the plight of refugees in Kosovo in 1999. During that year, which also marked the 300th birth anniversary of the Khalsa, Ravi saw the footage of Kosovan refugees on the news and was inspired by the Sikhi ideology of "Sarbat da Bhalla," meaning "well-being for all." This ideology recognizes the humanity in all people and calls for reaching out to those in need, regardless of race, religion, or borders. Ravi read about a small group that was organizing an aid convoy to Albania, and he phoned them to join them and deliver aid donated by the Sikh community. Within two weeks, they were on their way with two trucks and a van load of aid, and Khalsa Aid was born. Although the inspiration for the charity stems from a strong belief in Sikh principles, their work is not limited to the Sikh community. Khalsa Aid became the first-ever cross-border international humanitarian aid organization based on Sikh principles. Since 1999, Khalsa Aid has provided crucial aid to millions of people worldwide, from victims of the Yemen Civil War to refugees landing on the shores of Greece from the Middle East and the Rohingya Muslims from Myanmar seeking refuge in Bangladesh. They have also helped those affected by earthquakes, floods, and hurricanes in Nepal, Australia, and the Caribbean. In the UK, Khalsa Aid has helped residents cope with devastating floods and supported families who escaped the fire at Grenfell Tower in London.

KHALSA AID PROJECTS DONE SO FAR

JALANDHAR FAMILIES LOSE THEIR HOMES

Khalsa Aid volunteers are currently providing emergency aid to at least 50 families in Jalandhar, Punjab (India) who lost their homes after they were demolished on Sunday, 9th December 2022. The families had been settled in the Latifpura area since the partition of India in 1947, but unfortunately, a court ruling resulted in them being made homeless. Local government and agencies failed to provide alternative accommodation or other arrangements before the demolition, leaving families with young children and elderly members standing in harsh winter conditions without their belongings and access to washroom facilities. Upon hearing about the situation, Khalsa Aid India volunteers immediately attended the site and provided crucial supplies such as warm clothing, tents, mattresses, temporary washrooms, and langar (hot meals). They will continue to assist until alternative accommodation is identified for the families. These families had already suffered the horrors of the partition in 1947 and were never allocated any land. They had settled in Latifpura and now, 75 years later, their future generations have been made homeless. The families have written to the Chief Minister of Punjab to express their grave concerns, and we hope that this issue can be resolved as soon as possible. Our thoughts and prayers are with the families in Latifpura.



PAKISTAN FLOODS

Flash floods have displaced over 33 million people, leaving one-third of Pakistan underwater.

In Pakistan, severe monsoon flooding has caused flash floods that have displaced more than 33 million people, leaving one-third of the country underwater. On August 28th, 2022, an emergency was declared in Pakistan due to the extent of the flooding. According to initial reports, approximately one-third of the country is currently underwater and the UN has described the flooding as a 'monsoon on steroids'. At least 1,100 people have died and around one million homes have been damaged by the floods, which have affected at least 110 districts across Pakistan. The Pakistani Prime Minister has described the floods as "probably the worst in the history of Pakistan". Khalsa Aid International has responded to this disaster by mobilising teams on the ground to carry out assessments of the needs of the flood victims. They have teams spread across numerous provinces who will be providing temporary accommodation, hot meals (Langar), medical supplies, sanitary items, and food packs. They are also working around the clock with local and international suppliers to secure vital aid supplies. To stay updated on the situation on the ground and learn how to assist their relief efforts, please follow their social media channels.





KHALSA AID INTERNATIONAL’S RESPONSE TO THE UKRAINE CRISIS

Khalsa Aid International (KAI) had been preparing to respond to the Ukraine crisis before the war actually broke out. Therefore, when the conflict began, KAI was able to respond quickly by deploying teams to the borders of the five neighboring countries. Within 24 hours, a team was established in Poland, and within 48 hours, teams were set up in Slovakia, Romania, Hungary, and Moldova. At the beginning of the crisis, KAI received numerous calls from Indian students who were trapped in Ukraine and trying to escape to the borders. KAI also received many calls from parents of these students in India who requested help with their children's evacuation, receiving more than 1500 calls in the first week alone. To assist these students, KAI contacted restaurant owners, volunteers, and transport companies to see what they could do. KAI also worked with local volunteers to provide hot meals to the students as they fled.

SUMMARY OF OPERATIONS

POLAND – MEDYKA/BUDOMEIRZ

khalsa aid international (kai) quickly responded to the ukraine crisis by establishing teams on the borders of the five neighboring countries. in poland, they initially focused on arranging transport for refugees crossing the border and then expanded to provide medical aid, shelter, and food. within two days, they set up a 24-hour langar provision to serve hot food to the refugees. as the situation in ukraine worsened, more ukrainian citizens fled to neighboring countries, resulting in long waits at the border. kai was approached by caritas to provide hot food to refugees waiting to cross into poland and extended their food provision to refugees in shelters and other ngo volunteers. kai is also looking to secure a warehouse in poland to serve as a central european hub for aid supplies to be distributed in ukraine.

HUNGARY (ZAHONY/LUSHANKA/VYLOK)/ SLOVAKIA (VYSNE NEMECKE)/ ROMANIA(SIRET)

Khalsa Aid International dispatched teams to monitor the need for aid provision at the borders of three countries. The local authorities were able to cope with the influx of Ukrainian refugees and international students, but KAI collaborated with local NGOs to assist in delivering food aid, shelter, and medical first aid assistance. As waiting times increased for refugees trying to fly out of Hungary, KAI shifted their operations to Budapest to provide assistance. MOLDOVA (TIRASPOL/PALANKA/CHISINAU)

initially, khalsa aid international operated at the tiraspol border in moldova, providing food, transportation, and medical assistance to refugees. they even helped a family with a newborn baby return to their home country. when the border closed, they moved their operations to palanca and continued to provide food aid and transportation with the help of local ngos. currently, they have doubled their food aid provision at this border and are expanding their operations by setting up another base in chisinau to assist the growing number of refugees leaving ukraine due to continued russian attacks in the south.

WATER FOR AFRICA – MALAWI

khalsa aid has recently installed over 23 freshwater boreholes in rural areas of malawi, which is recognized as one of the world's poorest countries with a poverty rate of over 50% and extreme poverty rate of over 20%, according to the world bank. these boreholes provide safe drinking water to local communities who previously relied on contaminated water sources such as rivers and shallow wells, which can cause diseases such as cholera, diarrhoea, dysentery, hepatitis a, typhoid, and polio. in fact, diarrhoea alone causes the deaths of over 800,000 people each year, but it is largely preventable if safe water, sanitation, and hand hygiene are addressed. by addressing these risks, the deaths of up to 300,000 children under 5 years old could be prevented each year. khalsa aid teams assess each site for the current water supply, the number of people affected, and the distance from the nearest safe water source before drilling and constructing the borehole and pump. the local villagers are also trained in basic maintenance to ensure sustainability of the water supply. Thanks to generous donations, khalsa aid has been able to provide fresh and clean drinking water to villages that previously had to travel long distances on foot to access water. khalsa aid is committed to continuing its sustainable efforts to support the people of malawi.




WFP AND KHALSA AID INTERNATIONAL STRIKE PARTNERSHIP TO SUPPORT MOST VULNERABLE IN TWO CRITICAL FOOD CRISES

The World Food Programme (WFP) has announced a partnership with Khalsa Aid International to support vulnerable school children in Yemen and refugees from the Tigray Crisis in Sudan. The partnership aims to help WFP maintain programs that provide vulnerable children and women with the nutrition they need to survive and thrive. Khalsa Aid International's contribution will go towards WFP's Healthy Kitchens program in Yemen, which supports collective kitchens that employ vulnerable members of the community to prepare fresh, healthy meals for almost 10,000 school children. In Sudan, the partnership will provide essential nutrition assistance to refugees who have fled the Tigray crisis in Ethiopia, particularly children under five and pregnant and breastfeeding women who are at risk of malnutrition. Ravi Singh, Founder and CEO of Khalsa Aid International, expressed his pride in providing vitally needed support to vulnerable populations in Yemen and Sudan and thanked WFP for their partnership. Through this partnership, Khalsa Aid has further extended its reach in feeding the hungry globally, and Singh envisions a long partnership to help feed the world's hungry.

CIVIL RIGHTS OF INDIAN FARMERS

Khalsa Aid International expresses deep concern for the safety and welfare of the farmers and peaceful protesters who are demanding the repeal of the three controversial agricultural laws in India. The organization is alarmed by the disturbing images circulating on social and Indian media of police brutality and state suppression of peaceful protests, resulting in serious injuries to protesters. As a global humanitarian aid organization, Khalsa Aid International appeals to the Indian authorities to adhere to its constitution and international humanitarian standards to enable peaceful protests to continue. The organization requests that the Indian government maintain discipline, peace, and adherence to the rule of law at all times. Many members of the Indian diaspora worldwide are becoming increasingly anxious for their family members in India and are deeply concerned about their safety and well-being due to the indiscriminate violence from the authorities.

PUNJAB FLOODS

The recent severe floods in Punjab, India, have caused widespread devastation, with over 300 villages being directly affected and in urgent need of emergency aid. Khalsa Aid's India team has responded to the disaster, and the organization expresses gratitude for the continued support received. The floods have damaged roads, disrupted communication networks, and left many people stranded in their villages. Khalsa Aid International has established a team of rescue specialists in the region to provide emergency aid and assistance to the victims of the disaster. The organization has set aside £150,000.00 as emergency funds to respond directly to the crisis. Khalsa Aid International is now appealing for a further £150,000.00 to be raised, which will go towards the recovery, rebuilding, and clean-up of the affected region.
