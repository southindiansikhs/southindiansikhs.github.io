YUVRAJ SINGH

Yuvraj Singh, who was born on December 12, 1981, is a retired Indian cricketer who represented his country in all formats of the game. He was an accomplished all-rounder, known for his left-handed middle-order batting and slow left-arm orthodox bowling. Yuvraj's outstanding performances in One Day International (ODI) cricket earned him seven Player of the Series awards, which is the joint third-highest for an Indian, alongside former Indian captain Sourav Ganguly. Additionally, he is the son of former Indian fast bowler and Punjabi actor, Yograj Singh.









EARLY LIFE

Yuvraj Singh was born into a Punjabi Sikh family to Yograj Singh, a former Indian cricketer, and Shabnam Singh. As a child, he had a keen interest in playing tennis and roller skating, and displayed remarkable proficiency in both sports. He even won the National Under-14 Roller Skating Championship. However, his father discarded his medal and encouraged him to focus on cricket instead. Yuvraj's father took him to practice every day.

Yuvraj attended DAV Public School in Chandigarh and subsequently earned a degree in commerce from DAV College, Panjab University, Chandigarh. In addition to his academic pursuits, he had a brief stint as a child actor, appearing in minor roles in the Punjabi films "Mehndi Sagna Di" and "Putt Sardara."







YOUTH CAREER

Yuvraj's cricketing journey began when he made his debut for Punjab Under-16s at the tender age of 13 years and 11 months in November 1995. The following season, he was elevated to the Punjab Under-19s team, where he impressed by scoring an unbeaten 137 against Himachal Pradesh Under-19s. Yuvraj went on to make his first-class debut for Punjab in the Ranji Trophy against Orissa towards the end of 1997. However, he was dismissed for a duck while opening the innings.

In the 1999 Cooch Behar Trophy Final against Bihar, Yuvraj exhibited his immense potential by scoring a colossal 358 runs for Punjab after Bihar were all out for 357. Later that year, he played for India Under-19s against Sri Lanka Under-19s and made a significant contribution by scoring 89 runs from just 55 balls in the third ODI. Yuvraj continued to make his mark in domestic cricket, scoring an impressive 149 runs against Haryana in the 1999-2000 Ranji Trophy season.

2007 WORLD TWENTY20 AND VICE-CAPTAINCY

Yuvraj had the honor of serving as the vice-captain of the Indian team during the inaugural ICC World Twenty20 held in South Africa. In a Super 8 match against England at Durban, he achieved an extraordinary feat by hitting six sixes in an over off Stuart Broad. This outstanding performance allowed him to score the fastest fifty ever in a Twenty20 game, reaching the milestone off just 12 balls, which is still the fastest in any form of international cricket. It is noteworthy that this was only the fourth time that six sixes had been hit in one over in senior cricket, the first time in Twenty20 cricket, and the first time in any form of international cricket against a bowler from a Test playing nation. After his splendid innings, he finished with 58 runs off 16 balls and was awarded the man of the match title.

Yuvraj was unfortunately unable to play in the game against South Africa due to a minor injury. However, he recovered in time for the semifinal match against Australia. In this game, he made the highest runs by scoring 70 off just 30 balls and also hit the longest six of the tournament, which measured 119 meters (390 ft), off the bowling of Brett Lee. This outstanding performance was later recognized as the Best Twenty20 Batting Performance of 2007 by ESPN Cricinfo. Yuvraj was awarded yet another man of the match title as India won the match. In the final against Pakistan, he was dismissed for 14 runs, but India managed to win by five runs and clinch the tournament. The BCCI rewarded all members of the winning Indian team with a cash prize of ₹80 lakh, while Yuvraj received an additional reward of ₹1 crore as well as a Porsche 911 from the BCCI vice-president Lalit Modi. Yuvraj was named in the 'Team of the Tournament' by Cricinfo for the 2007 T20I World Cup.

GOLDEN WORLD CUP

Yuvraj's outstanding performance at the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup was remarkable, as he scored 362 runs, which included one century and four fifties, and took 15 wickets. He won four Man of the Match awards, which is jointly the most along with Sri Lanka's Aravinda de Silva in 1996, South Africa's Lance Klusener in 1999, and his compatriot Rohit Sharma in 2019. He was also declared as the Player of the Tournament. Yuvraj became the first all-rounder to score 300-plus runs and take 15 wickets in a single World Cup. In India's match against Ireland, he became the first player to score 50 runs and take 5 wickets in a World Cup match. Additionally, he achieved his 100th ODI wicket with the dismissal of Wesley Barresi in the World Cup match against the Netherlands. In the quarter-finals against defending champions Australia, he scored 57 runs and took 2/44, which earned him the Man of the Match award.










ACHIEVEMENTS AND HONOURS

Yuvraj Singh has achieved numerous accolades throughout his cricketing career. Some of his notable achievements are:

 He was a key member of the Indian team that won the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011.

 He was awarded the Player of the Tournament at the 2011 Cricket World Cup, where he scored 362 runs and took 15 wickets.

 Yuvraj hit six sixes in an over in the 2007 ICC World Twenty20 match against England, which is a world record in Twenty20 cricket.

 He is one of the only three players in the world to have achieved the rare feat of scoring a fifty and taking five wickets in an innings in the Cricket World Cup.

 He has won seven Player of the Series awards in ODI cricket, which is the joint third-highest by an Indian.

 Yuvraj was awarded the Arjuna Award in 2012, which is India's second-highest sporting honor.

 He was also conferred with the Padma Shri award, India's fourth-highest civilian award, in 2014.

 Yuvraj is the first Indian cricketer to hit six sixes in an over in international cricket and also the first player to achieve the rare feat of scoring a fifty and taking five wickets in an innings in a World Cup match.

 He has scored 8,701 runs and taken 111 wickets in ODI cricket, as well as 1,177 runs and taken 28 wickets in Test cricket.

 In IPL, he has played for multiple teams and won the Player of the Tournament award in the 2014 season while playing for the Kings XI Punjab.
