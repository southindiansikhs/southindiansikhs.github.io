MILKHA SINGH

Milkha Singh, also known as "The Flying Sikh," was an Indian sprinter in track and field who was introduced to the sport while serving in the Indian Army. He achieved a remarkable feat of winning gold in the 400 meters event at both the Asian Games and the Commonwealth Games, a feat that has never been matched by any other athlete. Additionally, he won gold medals at the 1958 and 1962 Asian Games. He represented India in the 1956 Summer Olympics in Melbourne, the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome, and the 1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo. In recognition of his outstanding sporting achievements, he was honored with the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award.



EARLY LIFE

Milkha Singh was born on November 20, 1929, in a Saini Sikh family. His birthplace was Govindpura, a village located 10 kilometers (6.2 miles) from Muzaffargarh city in Punjab Province, which was then a part of British India and is now Muzaffargarh District in Pakistan. He was one of fifteen siblings, out of which eight had passed away before the Partition of India. Unfortunately, he was orphaned during the Partition when his parents, a brother, and two sisters were killed in the violence that occurred between the villagers and Muslims who attempted to convert them. He was a witness to these gruesome incidents.







INTERNATIONAL CAREER

Singh competed in the 200m and 400m events at the 1956 Melbourne Olympic Games, representing India. However, due to his lack of experience, he did not progress beyond the heats. Nonetheless, his encounter with the eventual 400m champion, Charles Jenkins, proved to be a source of inspiration for him and also provided him with valuable insights into training techniques.

In 1958, Singh participated in the National Games of India held at Cuttack, where he set records for the 200m and 400m events, and subsequently won gold medals in the same events at the Asian Games. He then went on to win the gold medal in the 400m (440 yards at that time) competition at the 1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games, clocking an impressive time of 46.6 seconds. This historic achievement made him the first gold medalist from independent India at the Commonwealth Games. Prior to Vikas Gowda's triumph in 2014, Milkha Singh remained the only Indian male to have won an individual athletics gold medal at the Commonwealth Games.

Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first prime minister, persuaded Singh to set aside the traumatic memories of the Partition era to participate in a race against Abdul Khaliq in Pakistan in 1960. Following the race, a comment made by the then General Ayub Khan earned Singh the moniker of The Flying Sikh.

According to some sources, Singh set a world record of 45.8 seconds in France shortly before the 1960 Rome Olympics. However, the official report of the Games attributes the world record to Lou Jones, who had run 45.2 seconds at the 1956 Los Angeles Olympics.

At the Rome Olympics, Singh narrowly missed out on a medal in the 400m event, finishing in fourth place. He had beaten all the leading contenders except Otis Davis, and a medal had been anticipated given his excellent form. However, Singh made a crucial error when leading the race at the 250m mark by slowing down, believing that he could not maintain his pace. He also looked back at his fellow competitors, which cost him precious time. As a result, Davis, Carl Kaufmann and Malcolm Spence overtook him, resulting in a photo-finish. While Davis and Kaufmann both clocked a world-record-breaking time of 44.9 seconds, Spence and Singh finished with times of 45.5 and 45.6 seconds, respectively, both of which were under the pre-Games Olympic record of 45.9 seconds set by George Rhoden and Herb McKenley in 1952.

The Age newspaper noted in 2006 that "Milkha Singh is the only Indian to have broken an Olympic track record. Unfortunately, he was the fourth man to do so in the same race." However, the official Olympic report notes that Davis had already equaled the Rhoden/McKenley Olympic record in the quarter-finals and surpassed it with a time of 45.5 seconds in the semi-finals. Singh considers his errors in the race to be his "worst memory" and a missed opportunity for a medal.

ACHIEVEMENTS

Milkha Singh, also known as "The Flying Sikh," was an Indian track and field sprinter who competed in the 1950s and 1960s. He is considered one of India's greatest athletes and his achievements include:

Winning four gold medals in the Asian Games: Milkha Singh won gold medals in the 200 meters and 400 meters races at the 1958 Asian Games in Tokyo, and also won gold medals in both events at the 1962 Asian Games in Jakarta.

Setting a national record in the 400 meters: At the 1960 Olympic Games in Rome, Milkha Singh set a national record of 45.73 seconds in the 400 meters, a record that stood for almost 40 years.

Winning a gold medal in the Commonwealth Games: Milkha Singh won a gold medal in the 440 yards race at the 1958 Commonwealth Games in Cardiff.

Winning multiple medals in international competitions: Milkha Singh won several medals in international competitions, including the gold medal in the 400 meters at the 1958 and 1962 Asian Games, a gold medal in the 400 meters at the 1962 Commonwealth Games, and a gold medal in the 400 meters at the 1966 Asian Games.

Being honored with numerous awards and titles: Milkha Singh was awarded the Padma Shri in 1959, the Padma Bhushan in 1971, and was named the "Greatest Athlete" of the 20th century by the Indian Olympic Association in 2000.

Milkha Singh's life story was also depicted in a biographical sports drama film, "Bhaag Milkha Bhaag," which was released in 2013.
