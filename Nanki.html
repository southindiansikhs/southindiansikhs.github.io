BEBE NANKI JI

Bebe Nanaki, the elder sister of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, was born in 1464 in the village of Dera Chahal, which is now in the Lahore district of Pakistan's Punjab province. She was the daughter of Mehta Kalu and Mata Tripta. The Sakhis, which are historical and religious anecdotes, reflect Guru Nanak's deep love for his sister. In Punjabi folklore, a sister's love for her brother is a common theme, and there are many stories of Nanaki's profound and devoted affection for Nanak. Being five years older than Guru Nanak Dev Ji, she was the first to recognize his spiritual eminence and became his disciple. She shielded Nanak from their father's anger and disappointment, which he often faced. Nanaki was with him throughout his early childhood. In 1475, when Guru Nanak was only six years old, Nanaki got married to Jai Ram, a revenue official of good reputation, in Sultanpur, which is currently in the native state of Kapurthala and was then the capital of the Jalandhar Doab.

Nanak, being a rebellious soul, refused to conform to any unnecessary societal norms. He preferred to spend time in the company of wise saints and would generously give money to the poor and hungry. His father was unable to control him and make him adopt a respectable position in their village. Consequently, at the age of fifteen, Nanak was sent to live with his sister and work for her husband, Jai Ram.

Jai Ram was responsible for arranging Nanak's marriage to Sulakhani, the daughter of Moolchand Chand Khatri and Mata Chando of the village Pakhoke, District Gurdaspur. Bebe Nanaki, who was childless, adored her brother and considered herself blessed when he came to work for the Nawab and lived with her in Sultanpur. She arranged Guru Nanak's marriage and loved his sons, Sri Chand and Lakhmi das, as her own. Guru Nanak reciprocated her affection and after he quit the Nawab's service to preach his message, he would visit Sultanpur and meet his sister whenever possible.

Bebe Nanaki, the elder sister of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, holds a special place in Sikh history and played a vital role in spreading Sikhism. She was the first disciple of Guru Nanak and is considered one of the prominent women in Sikh history. Born in 1464, five years before Guru Nanak, to Mata Tripta and Mehta Kalu in Talwandi, now called Nankana Sahib in Pakistan, Bebe Nanaki was born at her maternal grandfather's house in village Chahal, district Lahore. Her maternal grandparents loved her and named her Nankian, which means "house of maternal grandparents." She was raised in a loving atmosphere by her father, a well-to-do revenue officer in the village. Her sharp features, round mouth, and cheerful face attracted everyone.

Bebe Nanaki played with her brother Nanak, took him shopping, and looked after his comforts. From an early age, Nanak rebelled against groundless rituals and superstitions. Bebe Nanaki was the first to recognize that Nanak was not an ordinary child but a man of God. She was the first follower of Guru Nanak and stood between Nanak and their parents when they got angry with him, telling them to recognize his true worth. For Bebe Nanaki, Nanak was not only her brother (Veer) but also her Guru (Peer).

In 1518, Bebe Nanaki sensed her end was near and detained Guru Nanak Dev Ji for a short while. She passed away in his presence, and three days later, her husband Jai Ram also passed away. Guru Nanak Dev Ji performed their obsequies. Bebe Nanaki arranged Guru Nanak's marriage and loved his sons, Sri Chand and Lakhmi das, as her own. Guru Nanak reciprocated her affection and visited her often, even after he had quit the Nawab's service to preach his message.

When Nanak Ji went to the river for a bath and didn't return for two days, people believed he had drowned, but Bebe Nanaki insisted that Nanak had been born to save people and couldn't drown. Bebe Nanaki was married at the age of eleven in 1475 to Bhai Jai Ram, a revenue officer in Sultanpur under Nawab Dault Khan Lodhi. In those days, early marriage was customary. Five years after her marriage, she started living with her husband, and Nanak missed her greatly. He lost interest in worldly affairs and became engrossed in meditation. Bebe Nanki also longed for her brother's company, and she persuaded her husband to find a job for Nanak in Sultanpur so that she could spend time with him. As a result, Bhai Jai Ram arranged for Nanak to work as a storekeeper for the Nawab of Sultanpur in 1485. In this way, she acted as an elder sister to help her younger brother settle in life. Bebe Nanki and her husband then found a suitable match, Mata Sulakhni, for Nanak, and they celebrated the wedding in 1487 with everyone's approval. The wedding party traveled from Sultanpur to Batala, Gurdaspur District, and back to Sultanpur. After the wedding, Nanak lived separately in Sultanpur, where his sister provided him with a large house. This demonstrates how Bebe Nanki willingly played the role of an elder sister in assisting her younger brother. This tradition of elder siblings helping younger ones is still part of our culture today. It also demonstrates the sisterly love that Bebe Nanki had for her brother. Guru Nanak Dev Ji also expressed this type of love in one of his hymns on page 935 of the Guru Granth Sahib Ji, where he says that when the brother (soul) departs, the sister (body) burns in separation. Bebe Nanki had a good and friendly relationship with her sister-in-law, Mata Sulakhni, and helped her raise her sons, Sri Chand and Lakhmi Chand. As she had no children of her own, she even adopted Sri Chand and looked after her needs, ensuring she never felt alone. Once, Sulakhni's mother complained to Bebe Nanki that her brother was not keeping his wife happy. Bebe Nanki reprimanded her and convinced her that there was nothing to complain about. Thus, Nanki was also a great support in Nanak's family life.

When Nanak Ji had successfully managed his accounts to everyone's satisfaction, he planned to begin his missionary work to spread his message throughout the world. Bebe Nanki assured him that she would take care of his family in his absence and did not discourage him. In fact, it was Bebe Nanki who inspired the idea of expressing devotion to God through music. She recognized her brother's musical talent and persuaded Mardana to accompany him, even purchasing a Rebab (a stringed musical instrument) for him. This tradition of reciting hymns with the accompaniment of musical instruments still prevails in Sikhism and has played a significant role in its preaching. We cannot overlook Bebe Nanki's role in the spread of Sikhism.

Bebe Nanki removed all obstacles that hindered Guru Nanak Dev Ji from preaching his mission. When Guru Nanak Dev Ji spent several years spreading his religion worldwide, Bebe Nanki took care of his parents, wife, and nephews during his absence. This is a prime example of how siblings prove to be the main pillars of help to each other in our culture. These values are an integral part of our culture, and the credit goes to Bebe Nanki. Upon Guru Nanak Dev Ji's return to Sultanpur at the end of his second tour, Bebe Nanki was unwell and asked him to stay for a few days. Unfortunately, she passed away soon after, and Guru Nanak Dev Ji performed her funeral rites. Bebe Nanki lived in Sultanpur for approximately forty years.

A tree that she planted still provides shade to people, and there is also a well that she had constructed for the people's benefit. Anybody would be proud to have a sister like Bebe Nanki. She was Guru Nanak Dev Ji's first disciple and the first person to recognize his holiness. There is no doubt that the first Gursikh was none other than Bebe Nanki. She explained the Guru's mission to her parents like a wise daughter and protected Guru Nanak Dev Ji from their anger. She treated her brother like God and played an essential role in his mission. Bebe Nanki's contribution to the spread of Sikhism is unique and worthy of praise.

Gurdwara Sri Bebe Nanaki Sultanpur Lodhi, Kapurthala, Punjab, India. Associated with Bebe Nanaki Ji. Gurdwara Sri Bebe Nanaki, constructed in 1970's, honours the memory of Bebe Nanaki, elder sister of Guru Nanak Dev Ji.

Gurdwara Sri Janam Asthan Bebe Nanaki Sultanpur Lodhi, Kapurthala, Punjab, India. Associated with Sri Guru Nanak Sahib Ji, Bebe Nanaki Ji. Gurdwara Sri Janam Asthan Bebe Nanaki marks the site where Bebe Nanaki (Sri Guru Nanak Sahib Ji's elder sister) was born.
